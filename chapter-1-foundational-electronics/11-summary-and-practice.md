# Summary and Practice

## Quick Recap of All Concepts

### 1. Charge and Atoms
- Atoms have **protons** (+), **neutrons** (neutral), and **electrons** (-)
- **Protons are stuck** in the nucleus and cannot move
- **Electrons can move** - this is the basis of electricity
- Metals have **free electrons** that can flow
- Electricity is the **movement of electrons**

### 2. Electric Field
- **Electric field** = invisible zone of force around charged objects
- Similar to gravity, but can attract OR repel
- Field lines point from **positive (+) to negative (-)**
- Fields get **weaker with distance**
- Voltage creates electric fields that push electrons

### 3. Ions and Charge Separation
- **Ion** = atom that lost or gained electrons
- **Positive ion**: lost electrons (more protons than electrons)
- **Negative ion**: gained electrons (more electrons than protons)
- **Charge separation** creates voltage
- Chemical reactions can transfer electrons

### 4. Battery Working
- Battery uses **chemical reactions** to separate charges
- **Anode (-)**: electron build-up
- **Cathode (+)**: electron shortage
- **Electrolyte**: blocks electrons, allows ions
- Maintains voltage by continuously separating charges
- Like a **water pump** that keeps circulation going

### 5. Voltage
- **Voltage = electrical pressure**
- Always measured **between two points**
- Unit: **Volts (V)**
- Battery creates voltage through charge separation
- Like **water tank height** (higher = more pressure)

### 6. Current
- **Current = flow of electrons**
- Unit: **Amperes (A)**
- Flows **through** components
- **Conventional current**: + to - (used in analysis)
- **Electron flow**: - to + (physical reality)
- Like **water flow rate** through a pipe
- Needs voltage AND complete path

### 7. Resistance
- **Resistance = opposition to flow**
- Unit: **Ohms (Œ©)**
- Caused by electron collisions with atoms
- Depends on: material, length, thickness, temperature
- Longer wire = more resistance
- Thicker wire = less resistance
- Like **pipe friction** or narrowness

### 8. Ohm's Law
- **V = I √ó R** (and rearrangements)
- More voltage ‚Üí more current
- More resistance ‚Üí less current
- Essential for all circuit calculations

### 9. Power
- **Power = energy used per second**
- Unit: **Watts (W)**
- **P = V √ó I** (also P = I¬≤R and P = V¬≤/R)
- Power converts to heat, light, motion, sound
- **I¬≤R heating**: power increases with square of current
- Components have power ratings

### 10. Units and Conversions
- Common prefixes: **k** (√ó1000), **m** (√∑1000), **Œº** (√∑1,000,000), **M** (√ó1,000,000)
- Factor of **1000** between adjacent prefixes
- Always convert to base units before calculations

## Key Formulas Summary

### The Essential Three

| Formula | Name | When to Use |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **V = I √ó R** | Ohm's Law | Find voltage from current and resistance |
| **I = V / R** | Ohm's Law | Find current from voltage and resistance |
| **R = V / I** | Ohm's Law | Find resistance from voltage and current |

### Power Formulas

| Formula | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|
| **P = V √ó I** | Know voltage and current |
| **P = I¬≤R** | Know current and resistance |
| **P = V¬≤/R** | Know voltage and resistance |

### Relationships

| Formula | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Q = I √ó t** | Charge = Current √ó Time |
| **I = Q / t** | Current = Charge / Time |
| **Efficiency = (P_out / P_in) √ó 100%** | Energy efficiency |

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

‚ö†Ô∏è **Forgetting to convert units** - Always use base units (A, V, Œ©, W) in calculations

‚ö†Ô∏è **Confusing voltage and current** - Voltage is pressure, current is flow

‚ö†Ô∏è **Wrong direction in Ohm's Law** - Use the triangle! I = V/R, not R/V

‚ö†Ô∏è **Ignoring power ratings** - Components can overheat and fail

‚ö†Ô∏è **Forgetting voltage drops** - LEDs drop ~2V, factor this in

‚ö†Ô∏è **Series vs parallel** - Know how resistances combine

‚ö†Ô∏è **Short circuits** - Very low resistance causes dangerous high current

‚ö†Ô∏è **Open circuits** - No complete path = no current
